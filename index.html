2. Additionally, data from Indonesia's Ministry of Environment and Forestry indicates that in 2023, the country generated 69.9 million tons of waste, with food waste accounting for 41.60%, plastic waste 18.71%, and household waste around 44.37%.
3. According to data reported by Liputan6.com, nearly 80% of the Bantar Gebang area is covered by waste piles. Tragically, this area remains a residential zone where locals live and earn a livelihood by scavenging.
4. This situation has significantly impacted the health of nearby communities, particularly in 2023, when air pollution levels rose due to the stench from garbage piles stretching up to 1.5 km from the site. This caused numerous children to suffer from respiratory infections (ISPA).
5. The total waste accumulation for 2024 is projected to be 31.9 million tons.

Solution

Sanbear Tech Innovators concluded 2 solutions to become a key that help solve the global problems.

  • Reuse means using something again instead of throwing it away. For example, instead of tossing out a glass jar, you can clean it and use it to store food or small items. Reusing helps reduce waste because it gives items a second life without needing to make or buy new ones.

  • Recycle means turning old or used items into something new. For instance, plastic bottles can be collected, melted down, and turned into new plastic products. Recycling helps reduce the amount of waste in landfills and saves resources by reusing materials to make new things.

Indonesia has also tried to solve these problems by announcing several laws regarding to waste management.

  • Law No. 18 of 2008: Regulates waste management and imposes penalties for improper disposal.

Recyper

Recyper stands for "recycle paper," which is the process of turning used paper into new sheets of paper.

Steps:

  1. Collect used paper.
  2. Cut the paper into small pieces and place them in a blender.
  3. Blend the paper until smooth, then add water and blend again to create a paper pulp. Add color if desired, and blend again until evenly mixed.
  4. Mix the paper pulp with water in a basin and stir well.
  5. Dip a paper screen into the basin, ensuring an even layer of pulp covers the screen, and then gently lift it out.
  6. Use a sponge to press the wet paper sheet onto a towel to remove excess water.
  7. Once the paper sheet releases from the screen, let it dry for at least overnight. The finished paper is now ready to use.

Organic Waste

Organic waste comes from living organisms such as humans, animals, and plants. It is biodegradable and can break down naturally. This type of waste is environmentally friendly and can be recycled into useful products. Examples include food scraps, vegetable leftovers, fruit peels, seeds, animal waste, and animal carcasses.

Organic waste is further divided into two types:
  1. Wet Organic Waste: Waste with a high water content, such as vegetable scraps, fruit seeds, or leftover fruits.
  2. Dry Organic Waste: Waste with low water content, such as wood, branches, dry leaves, and seashells

Positive Impacts of Organic Waste

  • Source of Compost Making: organic waste can be decomposed like food waste and can be used as an organic compost Organic Compost is useful for:
    • Land fertilizer.
    • Reduces the use of dangerous chemical fertilizers that will lessen environmental pollution and prevent various diseases caused by excessive chemicals.
    • Produces good nutrients as a source of food for plants

Negative Impacts of Organic Waste

  1. Health
    • Organic waste attracts pests like flies, which carry bacteria and breed in the waste, leading to diseases such as:
      • Parasitic diseases like hookworm and roundworm.
      • Bacterial diseases like diarrhea, plague, and typhoid fever.
      • Viral diseases like Hepatitis A and gastroenteritis.
  2. Environment
    • Creates unsanitary environments: Piles of organic waste attract insects, cause unpleasant odors, and lead to unsightly surroundings, especially near waste disposal sites.
    • Air pollution: The decomposition of organic waste emits foul odors, contributing to air pollution.

Compost Making Process

  1. Collect organic waste (food scraps, plant roots, old leaves, etc.).
  2. Crush the waste into smaller pieces to speed up decomposition.
  3. Mix it with EM4 solution (a mix of bacteria and fungi) to accelerate decomposition.
  4. Keep it in an airtight container to prevent disruption of the decomposition process.
  5. Wait 2-3 weeks, stirring the mixture 3 times a day.
  6. The result is solid and liquid compost that can be used to fertilize plants.

Inorganic Waste

Inorganic waste is made from materials that do not naturally decompose, such as plastics, metals, and glass. It takes a long time to break down and harms the environment.

  • Types of Inorganic Waste:
    • Solid Inorganic Waste: Hard materials like plastic, aluminum, and glass.
    • Liquid Inorganic Waste: Hazardous liquids, usually from industrial processes.
    • Gas Inorganic Waste: Gases like factory emissions that pollute the air.
  • Characteristics of Inorganic Waste:
    • Difficult to decompose.
    • Made from synthetic or man-made materials.
    • Can be recycled.

Positive Impacts of Inorganic Waste

  • As a future business idea. Inorganic waste can be useful if managed properly, one of ways is by recycling it. There are workshops such as ‚ÄúTrash 2 Move‚Äù which have developed a recycling business that turns inorganic waste into furniture, such as chairs, tables, bookshelves, etc.

Negative Impacts of Inorganic Waste

  • Inorganic waste that is difficult to decompose naturally becomes pollution for the environment.
    • Water pollution: plastic waste that accumulates and is dumped into the ocean endangers many marine animals that die because they become entangled in plastic. In addition, the plastic that is discarded eventually breaks down into microplastics that can enter the human body through the seafood consumed.
    • Soil pollution: plastic and styrofoam thrown away on the ground can cause soil pollution because these materials release harmful toxins. This can disrupt soil fertility and disturb the surrounding ecosystem.

Hazardous Waste (B3)

B3 waste is dangerous, toxic and can pollute the environment. This waste is the remainder of industrial activities and requires special processing to reduce or eliminate its dangerous toxic properties so that it does not pollute and endanger the environment. B3 waste must be collected and sent to the final processing site by an authorized government agency to ensure its safety. Examples include floor cleaners, batteries, glue, glass cleaners, used air fresheners, and other materials, most of which are made from chemicals.

  • According to Kompas, these are the characteristics of B3 waste:
    • Reactive and explosive.
    • Toxic and infectious.
    • Flammable or may cause fire.
    • Corrosive and dangerous for the environment because it can cause pollution

    B3 Waste Processing

    1. Stabilization method
        2. Adds chemicals mixed with B3 waste to minimize the speed of movement of B3 waste so that it does not pollute the environment. The addition of these chemicals is used to bind or change hazardous materials in waste and this method is usually used in liquid waste processing.
    2. Solidification processing method
        3. Adds additives to reduce the level of toxic substances in waste. The waste that uses this process is usually lime, cement and thermoplastic materials.
    3. Incineration processing method
        4. Reduces the volume of B3 waste which is usually used for hospital waste or medical waste. This method uses high temperatures so that the waste can be destroyed.
    4. Thermal processing method
        5. Similar to the incineration method, which uses very high temperatures. The difference is that the waste being processed is waste with very high levels of toxicity and is very dangerous. Apart from that, the supervising officer must also monitor the gas emissions produced so that they do not cause new pollution.
    5. Bioremediation processing method
        6. Uses bacteria or microorganisms to reduce toxins in waste. These enzymes and bacteria are used to break down toxic waste. The advantage of this method is that it is environmentally friendly because the bacteria and enzymes used are natural, but this method requires a long time on a small scale.
